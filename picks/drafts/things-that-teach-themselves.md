# Things That Teach Themselves

These sites don't explain things — they let you feel how they work: drag a gear, trace a logic chain, fly through 3 million papers, follow a historical domino.

# 自己会教你的网站

不是读说明书，是亲手感受它怎么运作：拖动齿轮、追溯因果链、飞越三百万篇论文、看一场历史的多米诺骨牌倒下去。

## Sites

### Mechanical Watch

- **URL**: https://ciechanow.ski/mechanical-watch
- **EN**: A masterclass in learning by touching — drag the gears, spin the balance wheel, feel the escapement click into place. You don't read about how a watch works; you operate one.
- **ZH**: 拖动发条、转动游丝、感受擒纵机构的咬合——你不是在"学"机械表，你是在亲手拆开和重组它。Bartosz Ciechanowski 出品，每一帧交互都是精心设计的理解时刻。

### How Browsers Work

- **URL**: https://howbrowserswork.com
- **EN**: Every time you type a URL, a hundred invisible steps happen before a pixel hits your screen. This site makes them all visible — animated, interactive, and in order.
- **ZH**: 每次输入网址，屏幕亮起之前发生了一百个你看不见的步骤。这个网站把它们全部动画化、交互化，一步步摊开在你面前。不只是前端工程师的专属——任何对浏览器原理感到好奇的人，都能在这里第一次真正看懂它。

### Going from 1+1=2 to Quantum Mechanics

- **URL**: https://quantum.schols.io/intro
- **EN**: A UCLA student built the guide they wished existed — no skipped steps, no assumed knowledge, every concept derived from first principles. Quantum mechanics as a thing you can actually follow.
- **ZH**: 一位 UCLA 学生写的"我当初希望存在的量子力学教程"：从算术讲到量子纠缠，没有一个知识跳跃，每个公式都从头推导。被吓退过量子力学课的人专属。

### Cateno

- **URL**: https://cateno.app
- **EN**: History doesn't happen in isolation — Cateno lets you trace how one event caused the next, pulling you through the chain of cause and effect that led to the world as it is.
- **ZH**: 历史不是孤立的事件，Cateno 让你沿着因果链条往回追溯：这件事为什么发生？它又导致了什么？拉着你一路走进历史的纵深。

### Worldview Upgrader

- **URL**: https://upgrader.gapminder.org
- **EN**: You think you know how the world works. This site respectfully disagrees — it surfaces the gaps between what most people believe and what the data actually shows.
- **ZH**: 你以为你了解这个世界。Gapminder 用数据温和地戳破常见的认知泡沫：贫困在减少还是增加？人口趋势是什么走向？你的直觉有多准？

### Trails

- **URL**: https://trails.pieterma.es
- **EN**: What if 100 books could map their own connections? Trails finds the invisible threads — "useful lies," "invisible fractures" — that run between different works, and lets you follow them.
- **ZH**: 如果一百本书可以自己画出彼此的联系会怎样？Trails 用 AI 发现文学作品之间的隐秘主题线索，把书变成一张可以漫游的思想地图。

### TomeSphere Atlas

- **URL**: https://tomesphere.com/atlas
- **EN**: Three million arXiv papers, mapped as a galaxy you can fly through. The shape of human scientific knowledge — where topics cluster, where fields converge — becomes something you can actually see.
- **ZH**: 将近三百万篇 arXiv 论文被映射成一片可以穿梭的星系。人类科学知识的形状——哪些领域聚集、哪些方向交汇——在这里变成了你可以亲眼看见的东西。

### ResearchRabbit

- **URL**: https://researchrabbitapp.com
- **EN**: Drop in one paper, and the citation network blooms around it — who built on this work, what it was built on, and which papers grew up beside it. Literature review becomes a thing you explore, not endure.
- **ZH**: 输入一篇论文，它的引用关系网络就在你眼前展开：谁引用了它，它引用了谁，和它同期成长的文献有哪些。文献综述从苦差事变成了一场探索。
